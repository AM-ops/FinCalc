Time Value of Money - Overview

Key terms:

Initial amount - The initial investment amount, also called the present value.

Payment/Annuity - When a constant amount of money is paid (or is to be paid) at regular intervals over a period of time, it is called an annuity.

Accumulated value - This is the amount of money that has accumulated by the end of a specific period. It is equivalent to the sum of all regular payments and the initial amount as well as the accumulated interest. Also called the future value.

Present value - This is the current value of the investment.

Annuities - When \( n \) equal payments are made at regular time intervals over a period of time, it is called an annuity. The payments can be made at the end of each payment period or at the beginning. An ordinary annuity certain is an annuity where the series of regular payments take place at the end of each payment period for a fixed number of periods. An ordinary annuity due is an annuity where the series of regular payments take place at the beginning of each payment period for a fixed number of periods.

Interest is paid in arrears - The interest is accumulated at the end of each period. If an amount is invested at time \( t\) (beginning of period \( t + 1\)), the interest is paid at the end of period \( t + 1 \).

Interest is paid in advance - The interest is accumulated at the beginning of each period.

Simple Interest Single Investment

Assume that an amount \(C\) is invested for a specific period at an effective interest rate \(i\) per year. Then the accumulated value (\(AV\)) after (\(n\)) years is given by:

\( AV = C(1 + in) \)

If you are working in a period other than a year, replace \(i\) with \(\frac{i^{(p)}}{p}\) In this case \( n \) refers to the total number of periods ( \( n = tp \) )

Note that \(PV = C(1 + in)^{-1}\) is the Present Value of the Investment and \(AV = C(1 + in) \) is the Future Value of the investment.

Compound Interest Single Investment

Assume that an amount \(C\) is invested for a specific period at an effective interest rate \(i\) per year. Then the accumulated value (\(AV\)) after (\(n\)) years is given by:

\( AV = C(1 + i)^n \)

If you are working in a period other than a year, replace \(i\) with \(\frac{i^{(p)}}{p}\)

Note that \(PV = C(1 + i)^{-n}\) is the Present Value of the Investment and \(AV = C(1 + i)^n\) is the Future Value of the investment.

Annuities

Payment made at the end of a period with a constant amount

Assume an amount \( X \) is invested at the end of each year for \( n \) years. (The first investment is made at the end of year 1 and the last investment is made at the end of year \( n \), thus a total of \( n \) investments). Assume interest is paid in arrears and the effective interest rate is \( i \) per year. The accumulated value of the investment at the end of \( n \) years is given by:

\( AV = X[\frac{(1 + i)^n -1}{i}] \)

If you are working in a period other than a year, replace \(i\) with \(\frac{i^{(p)}}{p}\). In this case \( n \) refers to the total number of periods ( \( n = tp \) ).

Note that the present value of the above-mentioned investment is given by:

\( PV = AV(1 + i)^{-n} = X[\frac{1 - (1 + i)^{-n}}{i}] \)

Payment made at the start of a period with a constant amount

Assume an amount \( X \) is invested at the beginning of the each year for \( n \) years. (The first investment is invested at the beginning of year 1 and the last investment at the beginning of year \( n \), therefore a total of \( n \) investments). Assume interest is paid in arrears and the effective interest rate is \( i \) per year. The accumulated value of the investment at the end of \( n \) years is given by:

\( AV = X(1 + i)[\frac{(1 + i)^n -1}{i}] \)

If you are working in a period other than a year, replace \(i\) with \(\frac{i^{(p)}}{p}\). In this case \( n \) refers to the total number of periods ( \( n = tp \) ).

Note that the present value of the above-mentioned investment is given by:

\( PV = AV(1 + i)^{-n} = X(1 + i)[\frac{1 - (1 + i)^{-n}}{i}] \)

Annuities with Increasing Installment

The formula for the AV of Increasing Annuities In Arrears and In Advance are given below respectively:

\( AV = C(1 + i)^n + X[\frac{(1 + i)^{n} - (1 + j)^{n}}{i - j}] \)

Note the above mentioned formula includes an Initial Amount that was invested

\( AV = C(1 + i)^n + X(1 + i)[\frac{(1 + i)^{n} - (1 + j)^{n}}{i - j}] \)

For both formulae the \( j \) is the percentage increase in each period

Please note if an Initial Amount was invested with an Annuity add the following to the exisiting formulas:
\( C(1 + i)^n \)

So for an example if an Initial Amount was invested with a Constant Annuity which made Payments at the start of a Period and Interest was paid in advance then the formula will be:

\( C(1 + i)^n + X(1 + i)[\frac{(1 + i)^n -1}{i}] \)
